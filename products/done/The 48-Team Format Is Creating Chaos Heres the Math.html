Scene 02 How 3-team groups work — the format explained simply
Voiceover
In a normal World Cup, four-team groups mean six matches, simultaneous final kickoffs, and two teams advancing. In 2026, each group of three plays just three matches. One team sits out each match day. There are no simultaneous final games. And the team resting on the last day gets a massive advantage watching their rivals battle from the sideline.

Scene 03 The collusion problem — two teams can collude in the final match

With three-team groups, the final match day features only two teams playing while the third watches. If a specific scoreline benefits both playing teams, they can silently agree to a result that eliminates the resting team. No simultaneous kickoffs means no safety net. The resting team is helpless, and FIFA has no answer for this flaw.

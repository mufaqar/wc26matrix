SCENE 04 How own goals actually happen — The mechanics explained
Voiceover
Let's understand how own goals actually happen. Most come from three situations. One: a cross or pass that a defender tries to clear but mishits. Two: a shot on target that a defender tries to block but redirects. Three: a high ball that bounces awkwardly off a defender's head or body. All three involve pressure. The defender isn't trying to score — they're trying to stop a goal and end up making it worse.

SCENE 05 Why this stat is so rare — Back-to-back own goals in WC history
Voiceover
Why has this never happened before? Because own goals are rare events. In 22 World Cups, there have been roughly 70 own goals total — about three per tournament on average. For the same team to benefit from two in consecutive matches requires an incredible coincidence. Opponents under pressure, unlucky bounces, and the US being in the right place to force mistakes. The probability is tiny.

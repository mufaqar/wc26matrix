SCENE 04 Recovery science basics — The 3 pillars of between-match recovery
Voiceover
Recovery science breaks down into three pillars. One — muscle glycogen replenishment. After a match, your muscles are empty. It takes 24 hours to fully refuel with proper nutrition. Two — inflammation management. Micro-tears in muscle fibers cause swelling. Ice baths, compression, anti-inflammatories help but only reduce about 30 percent of the damage. Three — central nervous system recovery. Your brain fires thousands of signals during a match. That mental fatigue is just as real as physical exhaustion. Ignore any pillar and recovery drops.

SCENE 06 Sleep and nutrition — The hidden recovery weapons
Voiceover
Sleep is the single most powerful recovery tool. Every hour of sleep below 8 hours increases injury risk by 15 percent. During deep sleep, your body releases growth hormone that repairs muscle tissue. But in a World Cup, players lose 2 to 3 hours of sleep per night — travel, late matches, adrenaline, noise. Nutrition is just as critical. A player burns 3,000 to 4,000 calories per match. If they don't replace those calories in the 24-hour window, the next match is played on empty reserves.

SCENE 07 Rotation strategy — How smart managers use their squad
Voiceover
This is where squad management becomes a superpower. A World Cup squad has 23 players. Only 11 start each match. That leaves 12 players on the bench. Smart managers rotate 3 to 4 players per match — resting key starters against weaker group opponents, saving stars for knockout matches. The math is simple: if you spread the 7 matches across 18 outfield players, each player averages 5.4 starts instead of 7. That's 1.6 fewer matches of wear per player. Rotation isn't a luxury in this World Cup. It's a necessity.

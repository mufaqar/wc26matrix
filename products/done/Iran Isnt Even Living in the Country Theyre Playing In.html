SCENE 1 Hook — The most bizarre travel story of WC26
SCENE 1 — Hook Iran is playing in the USA but living in Mexico
VOICEOVER
Iran is playing in the World Cup in the United States. But they are not allowed to live there. Instead, the entire team is based in Tijuana, Mexico, commuting across the border just to play their matches. This is the most bizarre travel arrangement of any team in World Cup history. Here is the story you have not heard.

SCENE 2 — The Ban Trump administration travel restrictions on Iranian nationals

Executive orders signed during the first term placed severe limits on Iranian citizens entering the United States. Despite the World Cup being hosted on American soil, Iran players, coaches, and staff were effectively barred from establishing a residence or training base inside the country.

SCENE 3 The logistics — daily commute from Tijuana to LA
SCENE 3 — Logistics The daily border crossing routine
VOICEOVER
Every match day, Iran team wakes up in Tijuana, boards a charter bus, crosses the US-Mexico border at San Ysidro, and drives 140 miles north to Los Angeles. Then they play a World Cup match. Then they get back on the bus and return to Mexico. It is a six-hour round trip on match days and they do it every single time.

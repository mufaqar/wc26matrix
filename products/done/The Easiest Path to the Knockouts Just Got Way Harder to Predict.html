SCENE 02 The new Round of 32 — 8 best third-place teams
Voiceover
The 2026 World Cup expanded to 48 teams, but only 32 advance to the knockout stage. Sixteen group winners and sixteen runners-up qualify automatically. Then the eight best third-place teams join them based on overall record. Your team does not need to win the group. They just need to be better than the other third-place teams. And that is where everything gets complicated.

SCENE 03 Step 1 — Points (3 for win, 1 for draw)
Voiceover
Step one is the foundation: points. Three for a win, one for a draw, zero for a loss. The eight third-place teams with the most points advance to the knockout stage. But here is where it gets interesting. Some groups will have third-place teams with four points while others only have one. Every single point from matchday one becomes critical for your team's survival.

SCENE 04 Step 2 — Goal difference (why every goal matters)
Voiceover
When points are tied, step two is goal difference. A 4-0 victory is not just a good result — it could be the margin that sends your team through. And a 2-1 loss is much better than a 3-0 loss. Every goal scored and conceded across all three group matches is recorded. Teams must attack even when already qualified, because those goals could save them later.

SCENE 05 Step 3 — Goals scored (attacking matters even in losses)
Voiceover
Still tied after goal difference? Step three is goals scored. The team that scored more goals overall advances. A losing team that scored three times might advance over a winning team that only managed one. This rule punishes defensive, cautious play. Teams must attack relentlessly — even when they are behind — because every goal in the loss column could become a lifeline.

SCENE 06 Step 4 — Team conduct score (fair play as a tiebreaker)
Voiceover
If everything else is equal, step four brings in the fair play score. Yellow cards cost minus one point. Red cards cost more. The team with fewer disciplinary points throughout the group stage advances. Imagine your team going home because a defender picked up too many yellow cards. Discipline matters more than ever in this format.

SCENE 05 1:12 — 1:30 The legal framework — Host city contracts explained
Voiceover
To understand why FIFA could say no, you need to understand host city contracts. When a city bids to host World Cup matches, it signs a legally binding contract with FIFA. This contract specifies exactly how many matches the city hosts, which stadiums are used, and what the city must provide. It includes penalty clauses — if a city backs out, FIFA demands compensation. And critically, match locations can only be changed by mutual agreement between FIFA and the host city. No president, no government, no third party can override it.

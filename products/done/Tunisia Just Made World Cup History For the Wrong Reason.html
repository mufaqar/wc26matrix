Scene 4 — Historical Rarity
Scene 04 Only 5 managers in 92 years of World Cup history have been fired mid-tournament before this. Tunisia made it 6.
Voiceover

In 92 years of World Cup football, only five managers had ever been fired during the tournament. Five. In twenty-two World Cups. Tunisia has now made it six. The list includes Spain's manager in 2014 after a 5-1 loss to Netherlands — sound familiar? — and Cameroon's coach in 1998. It is one of the rarest events in football. Tunisia joined that list in under 24 hours.

Scene 5 — Why Federations Sack Mid-Tournament
Scene 05 Panic? Accountability? Scapegoating?
Voiceover

Why do federations fire a coach in the middle of a World Cup? Three reasons. Panic — the hope that a new voice sparks a turnaround. Accountability — the federation signals to fans that someone will pay for failure. And scapegoating — a sacrifice to distract from deeper problems. Usually it is a mix of all three. And it rarely works.

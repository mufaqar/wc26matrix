A few standout answers to "who scored the max goals":

For a single tournament, nobody has come close to Just Fontaine's 13 goals in just six matches at Sweden 1958 — a record that's stood for nearly 70 years.

For career World Cup goals across multiple tournaments, the record was Miroslav Klose's 16 goals (2002–2014) — and right now, in this 2026 tournament, Messi has just tied that mark at 16 career World Cup goals, with his hat-trick against Algeria putting him level with Klose as the joint all-time leading scorer in World Cup history.
